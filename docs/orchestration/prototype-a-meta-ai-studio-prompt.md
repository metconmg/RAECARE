## Required Story Architecture

1. Describe the problem
   1.a. Care is being treated like gig work.
   1.b. Families are forced into unsafe search behavior.
   1.c. Caregivers are flattened into commodity labor.
   1.d. High-stakes care needs are mixed with low-stakes browsing.
   1.e. Marketplace logic creates pressure before trust is established.

2. Why nothing fixes it
   2.a. Ratings do not equal trust.
   2.b. Search does not understand care risk.
   2.c. Credential claims are not enough.
   2.d. Negative trust should not scale.
   2.e. Existing platforms optimize conversion, not protection.

3. Introduce RAECARE
   3.a. RAECARE is a trusted support network, marketplace, and care ecosystem.
   3.b. RAECARE is not a directory.
   3.c. RAECARE is a trust-governed matchmaking system.
   3.d. The public promise: care that is verified, matched, and yours.

4. RAECARE ethos and posture
   4.a. Protection before conversion.
   4.b. Mutual compatibility, not hiring pressure.
   4.c. Visibility must be earned.
   4.d. Dignity is preserved through non-punitive omission.
   4.e. Clinical boundary discipline: structured relay, no clinical chat.
   4.f. Visual posture: midnight navy, amber, Cormorant Garamond, institutional trust.

5. The how — high level
   5.a. Intake without open search.
   5.b. Caregiver trust pipeline.
   5.c. Localized trust graph.
   5.d. Match engine.
   5.e. Soft introduction.
   5.f. Boundary negotiation.
   5.g. Active care relationship.
